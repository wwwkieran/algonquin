Algonquin Park is 2,946 square miles of wilderness, larger than the total area of Delaware and Rhode Island combined.
The park contains over 2,400 lakes, making it a canoeist's paradise.
The lakes are connected by a network of 800 portages, enabling longer canoe trips deeper into the park.
I've done 10 canoe trips in Algonquin Park since 2008. I wanted to figure out how much of the park I've seen by different metrics.
Of the 385 miles of portages in Algonquin Park, I've seen about 24 miles (6.3%).
Of the 308 square miles of water in the park, I've seen roughly 167 square miles (54.3%).
Data from OpenStreetMap.
